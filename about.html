About Daniel Easterman

Daniel Easterman is a Home Affairs reporter with the Jewish Chronicle in London.

Prior to taking up the position he completed a Masters degree in Digital Journalism at Goldsmiths, University of London, and was awarded the mark of "distinction" for his practical thesis using remotely-piloted aircraft or "drones" to cover the issue of deforestation in the Highlands of Scotland.

As part of the degree Daniel also completed a two month industry placement at the Telegraph Media Group in London. During the placement he collaborated on an in-depth database project for the Telegraph's lifestyle section and published twelve articles for the foreign news and travel desks.

Before returning to London, Daniel worked as a freelance journalist in Israel for almost two years writing and providing news photography for the Times of Israel, the Jerusalem Post, 972mag.com and several online commercial publications.

Some of the wide-ranging issues he covered during these two years included environmental water policy, the West Bank, Israeli parliamentary politics and anti-government demonstrations in Tel Aviv.

Daniel also holds a Masters in Modern Jewish Studies and Israeli Politics from Oxford University and has had experience working in Israel’s Ministry of Economy (formerly Industry Trade and Labour) and the American Jewish Committee (AJC) in Washington DC.

In 2007 he received a First in International Relations from the University of Birmingham (UK)
